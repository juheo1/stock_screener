# Sandisk (NASDAQ:SNDK) Long Position Fundamental Analysis

Prepared: May 11, 2026, shortly after midnight Eastern time. Because the U.S. market is closed at this hour, the latest confirmed market price used here is the May 8, 2026 close.

This is a fundamental analysis memo, not personalized financial advice.

## Bottom Line

I would not chase a full new long position today if SNDK opens near or above the latest confirmed close of $1,562.34. Sandisk's fundamentals have dramatically improved, but the stock already reflects a very aggressive AI-memory supercycle scenario.

My estimated 12-month fundamental fair-value target is $1,550 per share. That is roughly in line with the latest close, so the base-case risk/reward is not attractive for a fresh full-size entry. My stretch/bull-case target is $2,000 if NAND pricing, datacenter SSD demand, and long-term customer agreements keep earnings power elevated into fiscal 2027.

A better risk-adjusted entry zone is $1,250 to $1,350. If you still want exposure today, a small starter position is more defensible than a full position, especially after the May 8 surge.

## Recent Fundamentals

Sandisk reported a breakout fiscal Q3 2026:

- Revenue: $5.95 billion, up 97% sequentially and 251% year over year.
- GAAP net income: $3.615 billion.
- Diluted GAAP EPS: $23.03.
- Non-GAAP diluted EPS: $23.41.
- GAAP gross margin: 78.4%, up sharply from 50.9% in fiscal Q2.
- Datacenter revenue: $1.467 billion, up 233% sequentially and 645% year over year.
- Edge revenue: $3.663 billion, up 118% sequentially and 295% year over year.
- Consumer revenue: $820 million, down 10% sequentially but up 44% year over year.

The most important detail is pricing. In the 10-Q, management said Q3 net revenue rose 251% year over year primarily because average selling price per gigabyte rose 248%, while exabytes sold were flat. That means the current profit level is powerful but still highly sensitive to NAND pricing.

## Demand Visibility

The strongest fundamental argument is not just one hot quarter. Sandisk now has meaningful long-term customer commitments:

- Contract liabilities increased to $511 million, mainly from customer advances tied to long-term agreements.
- Remaining performance obligations were $41.6 billion as of April 3, 2026.
- About 15% of those remaining performance obligations are expected to be recognized as revenue over the next 12 months.

This new business model can reduce the classic memory-cycle whiplash by locking in multi-year customer demand. It does not eliminate cyclicality, but it improves visibility versus pure spot-market exposure.

## Valuation

My base-case target uses a normalized forward EPS approach. Current quarterly earnings power is very high, but I do not want to simply annualize Q3 or Q4 guidance because NAND pricing can reverse quickly.

Assumptions:

- Normalized fiscal 2027 EPS: $150 per share.
- Fair multiple: 10x normalized EPS.
- Net cash per share: about $25.
- Rounded target to reflect uncertainty: $1,550.

Calculation:

```text
$150 normalized EPS x 10 = $1,500
+ $25 net cash per share = $1,525
Rounded 12-month base target: $1,550
```

Scenario range:

- Bear case: $700 to $950 if NAND pricing normalizes faster than expected, hyperscaler AI capex slows, or customers push back on contract pricing.
- Base case: $1,450 to $1,600 if fiscal 2027 EPS lands around $140 to $160 and the market assigns a 9x to 10x multiple.
- Bull case: $1,900 to $2,100 if Sandisk sustains $170 to $185 EPS power and investors value the business at 10.5x to 11x because long-term agreements reduce cyclicality.

## Investment View

Fundamentally, Sandisk is one of the clearest beneficiaries of tight NAND supply, AI datacenter storage demand, and higher-value enterprise SSD mix. The zero-debt balance sheet, $41.6 billion of remaining performance obligations, and $6 billion buyback all improve the investment case.

The issue is entry price. At $1,562.34, the stock is near the top of its 52-week range after a 16.6% one-day jump and a massive one-year move. The base-case valuation is already captured in the price. Buying here requires confidence that the bull case is becoming the base case.

Decision:

- New full long today: No, not attractive on base-case risk/reward near $1,560.
- Small starter position: Reasonable only for high-conviction investors who can tolerate memory-stock volatility.
- Better entry: $1,250 to $1,350.
- Base target: $1,550.
- Stretch target: $2,000.
- Fundamental invalidation watch: NAND ASP reversal, weaker AI datacenter capex, customer pushback on long-term contracts, Kioxia/Flash Ventures execution issues, aggressive industry capacity additions, or a guidance reset below the current fiscal 2027 earnings ramp.
