# Micron Technology (NASDAQ: MU) Long Position Fundamental Analysis

Prepared: May 11, 2026 US trading date.

This is a fundamental analysis memo, not personalized financial advice. The latest market quote I could verify was the May 8, 2026 close because May 11 trading/live data may change after this file is written.

## Bottom Line

I would not chase a full new long position today at roughly $746 to $757. Micron's fundamentals are exceptionally strong, but the stock has already moved almost vertically and now prices in a large part of the AI memory supercycle.

My estimated 12-month fundamental fair-value target is about $850 per share, with a stretch/bull case around $1,050 to $1,150 if AI memory shortages persist, HBM and data-center SSD demand stay undersupplied, and FY2027 earnings power approaches current sell-side expectations.

A better risk-adjusted entry zone is $625 to $675. At $746.81, upside to my base target is about 14%, while a normal memory-stock drawdown after a parabolic run could easily be 20% to 35%. If you still want exposure today, a small starter position is more defensible than a full-size entry.

## Recent Fundamentals

Micron reported record fiscal Q2 2026 results for the quarter ended February 26, 2026:

- Revenue: $23.86 billion, up from $13.64 billion in fiscal Q1 2026 and $8.05 billion a year earlier.
- GAAP net income: $13.79 billion, or $12.07 diluted EPS.
- Non-GAAP net income: $14.02 billion, or $12.20 diluted EPS.
- Operating cash flow: $11.90 billion, versus $3.94 billion a year earlier.
- GAAP gross margin: 74.4%; non-GAAP gross margin: 74.9%.
- Adjusted free cash flow: $6.9 billion after $5.0 billion of net capex.
- Cash, marketable investments, and restricted cash: $16.7 billion.

The fiscal Q3 2026 guide was even stronger:

- Revenue: $33.5 billion, plus or minus $750 million.
- Gross margin: about 81%.
- Non-GAAP EPS: $19.15, plus or minus $0.40.

The business-unit detail supports the AI-memory thesis:

- Cloud Memory revenue was $7.75 billion with 74% gross margin.
- Core Data Center revenue was $5.69 billion with 74% gross margin.
- Mobile and Client revenue was $7.71 billion with 79% gross margin.
- Automotive and Embedded revenue was $2.71 billion with 68% gross margin.

Micron also announced shipment of its 245TB 6600 ION data-center SSD on May 5, 2026. That product is aimed at AI, cloud, enterprise, and hyperscale workloads, and management says it reduces rack requirements by 82% versus HDD deployments for equivalent raw capacity. This is not the core of the whole valuation by itself, but it reinforces the broader point: Micron is benefiting from AI infrastructure bottlenecks in both memory and storage.

## Valuation

My base-case target uses a normalized forward EPS approach rather than only trailing EPS, because trailing EPS does not yet capture Micron's fiscal Q3 earnings run-rate.

Assumptions:

- Normalized forward EPS: $80 per share.
- Fair multiple: 10.5x, reflecting structurally better AI/HBM demand but still respecting Micron's cyclicality.
- Add net cash/investments after debt: about $6 per share.

Calculation:

```text
$80 normalized forward EPS x 10.5 = $840
+ about $6 net cash per share
= $846
Rounded target: $850
```

Scenario range:

- Bear case: $400 to $500 if memory pricing normalizes quickly, AI capex slows, or the market re-rates Micron as a peak-cycle commodity memory name again.
- Base case: $800 to $900.
- Bull case: $1,050 to $1,150 if FY2027 EPS power reaches roughly $95 to $105 and the market accepts a 10.5x to 11.0x multiple because AI demand makes the cycle less volatile.

## Investment View

Fundamentally, Micron is one of the clearest beneficiaries of the AI infrastructure buildout. The combination of HBM demand, data-center DRAM, enterprise SSDs, strong pricing, tight supply, and record margins creates a very real earnings inflection.

The problem is the entry price. At around $746.81 to $757.35, the stock is essentially at a 52-week high after a 15% one-day jump and a 37% weekly move. That is not a calm entry setup, even for a company with excellent fundamentals.

Decision:

- New full long today: No, not attractive on risk/reward.
- Small starter position: Reasonable only for high-conviction investors who can tolerate large volatility.
- Better entry: $625 to $675.
- Base target: $850.
- Stretch target: $1,050 to $1,150.
- Fundamental invalidation watch: DRAM/NAND spot-price reversal, HBM share loss to SK hynix or Samsung, customer pushback on memory pricing, AI capex slowdown, aggressive industry capacity additions, or a major margin rollover from current peak-like levels.
