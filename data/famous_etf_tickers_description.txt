Below is practical list of famous, high-liquidity ETFs that map well to the kinds of broad index exposures commonly seen inside retirement-oriented vehicles such as 401(k)s, 403(b)s, target-date structures, and many 529 plan lineups. In practice, those plans often use mutual funds, collective investment trusts, or plan-specific share classes rather than ETFs directly, but the underlying exposures are usually the same broad U.S. stock, international stock, and bond indexes.

SPY - SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust
Tracks the S&P 500, which is the classic large-cap U.S. equity benchmark and one of the most widely followed retirement core exposures. It is also one of the most heavily traded ETFs.

QQQ - Invesco QQQ Trust
Tracks the Nasdaq-100, giving concentrated exposure to large non-financial growth and technology-oriented companies. It is one of the most famous and most actively traded ETFs.

VTI - Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF
Tracks the broad U.S. stock market, not just the S&P 500, so it includes large-, mid-, and small-cap U.S. equities. This type of total-market exposure is commonly used in retirement portfolios.

IWM - iShares Russell 2000 ETF
Tracks U.S. small-cap stocks through the Russell 2000. This is a well-known way to add the smaller-company segment that is not fully represented by the S&P 500 alone.

VXUS - Vanguard Total International Stock ETF
Tracks broad non-U.S. equities across developed and emerging markets. This is a common international building block and also appears in 529-style index menu structures.

VEA - Vanguard FTSE Developed Markets ETF
Tracks developed markets outside the U.S. This is useful when you want international exposure without emerging markets. It is also a very liquid ETF.

IEMG - iShares Core MSCI Emerging Markets ETF
Tracks emerging-market equities and is one of the larger, more actively traded emerging-market core ETFs.

BND - Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF
Tracks the broad U.S. investment-grade bond market. This is one of the most standard bond exposures used in retirement allocation frameworks and official 529 index portfolios.

AGG - iShares Core U.S. Aggregate Bond ETF
Another broad U.S. core bond ETF, commonly used as the “aggregate bond market” benchmark. It is also highly liquid.

VNQ - Vanguard Real Estate ETF
Tracks U.S. REITs. Real estate is not always a default core holding in retirement plans, but it is a common “famous section” that many 401(k) menus and diversified portfolios include.

A simple way to think about the sections is:
- U.S. large cap: SPY
- U.S. growth / Nasdaq: QQQ
- U.S. total market: VTI
- U.S. small cap: IWM
- International total: VXUS
- International developed: VEA
- Emerging markets: IEMG
- U.S. bonds: BND or AGG
- Real estate: VNQ

* One important nuance: for actual 401(k), 403(b), Social Security-related retirement saving programs, or 529 plans, you often will not see these exact ETF tickers available to buy directly. Instead, the plan usually offers plan-specific index funds or target-date funds built on these same asset-class exposures.
