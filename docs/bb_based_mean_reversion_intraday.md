Needed indicators:
#1: SMA (20, C)
#2: BB (20, EMA, H, 2, 0)
#3: BB (20, WMA, H, 2, 0)
#4: BB (20, EMA, L, 2, 0)
#5: BB (20, WMA, L, 2, 0)

Fill between #2 & #3 -> Green band / take buy position.
	- This will result in upper and lower green bands.
Fill between #4 & #5 -> Red band / take sell position.
	- This will result in upper and lower red bands.

Buy side is based on the "high" because high-based BB react more to the top of the candles.
So when price pulls back, it tends to touch around the upper band a bit faster than a normal BB.
This is an aggresive setting to avoid missing buy entries in an uptrend.

Sell side is based on the "low" because low-based BB react more loosely to the bottom of the candles.
So when prices bounces, it reaches the lower band after rising a bit more than normal BB.
This is conservative setting to catch clear sell entries in down-trend.

Default BB and 20 moving average.
If price touches the lower band, and comes back inside the BB.
How strong the candles are when they break through the BB.
At the same time:
- if 20 moving avg points up, look for buy.
- if 20 moving avg points down, look for sell.

Ideal: 1H, 4H, daily all agree to indicate either buy or sell.
One or two of these is good enough too.
Then 1 or 5 min chart, take the same position.

If the higher time frame is bullish, we only enter when price touches the lower green band.
If the higher time frame is bearish, we only enter when price touches the higher red band.

Key: signal must come from the band that matches the higher time frame direction.

If 20 SMA is pointing up, and price touch the lower green band, take buy position.
If 20 SMA is pointing down, and price touch the higher red band, take sell position.

Example:
On 1 hr chart, if price touch lower normal BB, and closed back inside the band,
And on 5 min chart, 20 SMA slope is up, and price touch the lower green band,
take buy position with SL at the top of the lower red band, and TF set at twice the of the SL so that TF:SL ratio is 2:1.
